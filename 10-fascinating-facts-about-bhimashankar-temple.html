FAQs

Q: What is the history behind Bhimashankar Temple?

A: Bhimashankar Temple has a rich history that dates back to ancient times. It is believed to have been built during the 13th century by King Bhimdev of the Yadava dynasty. The temple has witnessed several renovations and additions over the years, contributing to its impressive architectural style.

Q: What is the significance of Bhimashankar Temple?

A: Bhimashankar Temple holds great religious significance for followers of Lord Shiva. It is considered one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, representing the manifestation of Lord Shiva in Hindu mythology. The temple is also associated with several mythological stories and is believed to be the place where Lord Shiva defeated the demon Tripurasura.

Q: Can visitors explore the surrounding natural beauty?

A: Yes, visitors can enjoy the breathtaking natural beauty surrounding Bhimashankar Temple. The temple is located in the Western Ghats, which is known for its lush greenery and diverse flora and fauna. There are several trekking trails in the area that allow visitors to explore the scenic landscapes and enjoy the serene ambiance.

Q: Are there any festivals celebrated at Bhimashankar Temple?

A: Yes, Bhimashankar Temple celebrates several festivals with great enthusiasm. The most prominent festival is Mahashivaratri, which is dedicated to Lord Shiva and attracts a large number of devotees. During this time, the temple is beautifully decorated, and special prayers and rituals are performed.

Q: Is there any dress code to enter Bhimashankar Temple?

A: While there is no strict dress code, it is advisable to dress modestly and respectfully when visiting Bhimashankar Temple. As it is a place of worship, it is recommended to wear clothes that cover the shoulders and knees as a mark of respect for the religious sentiments associated with the temple.
