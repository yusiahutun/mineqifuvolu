Antonio Brown 'FINALLY doles out $15,000 in child support payments after arrest for failing to pay':

August 2024 Â· 3 minute read

Antonio Brown has finally coughed up child-support money after reportedly paying out $15,000 to ex-girlfriend Wiltrice Jackson following his arrest and release from jail.

Brown spent the weekend in jail for allegedly neglecting to pay child support to Jackson - the mother of his 15-year-old daughter Antanyiah.

The 35-year-old was booked on Sunday before being released on $15,000 bond. 


According to TMZ Sports, the former NFL star has doled out the same sum in child support payments to Jackson after being released from jail.

Jackson alleged earlier this month that he owes her almost $31,000 while calling for police to follow through with his arrest.

'I do want him arrested,' she said. 'Because, right now, he's making a mockery out of the judge, out of everything, because he feels like he's untouchable.' 

A judge ruled last month that Brown had missed a child support payment of $15,000, which was the second time this year he had been accused of doing so.

In April, a judge made an identical ruling after the ex-Buccaneers and Steelers wide receiver was judged to have not made a $30,000 payment to Jackson. On that occasion, he paid up before any enforcement was needed.

In a video which appears to show his child-support payment, Brown could be heard singing some Young Jeezy rap lyrics which were seemingly aimed at Jackson.

'And I don't wanna tell my child that his moms ain't s***,' he belted out while holding the apparent receipt for his payment and a large wad of cash. 'And this ain't about me, she just a money hungry b***h.'

While also calling for his arrest, Jackson spoke of her desire for Brown to be more 'involved' in their daughter's life as she begins to receive track-and-field scholarship offers from major US schools.

'By him having a daughter like an athlete like her, you would think he'd be in her corner to support her,' Jackson said.

Brown is coming off a disastrous stint as a team owner in the National Arena League that ended with his club, the Albany Empire, having its franchise agreement terminated due to his failure to pay dues and fines.

Brown is coming off a disastrous stint as owner of National Arena League club the Albany Empire, whose franchise agreement was terminated due to his failure to pay dues and fines

As of last month, ESPN reported that team players were still owed compensation by Brown.

The former NFL receiver has previously been arrested and he's been accused of a number of other crimes as well, ranging from sexual and physical assault to destruction of property.

In January 2019, Brown was alleged to have pushed Jackson in a domestic dispute but he was not arrested, according to TMZ.
