Canadian actress Katie Douglas portrayed Sally Wilcox in The Ghost of the Villa (2013-2014). At the age of six, Douglas started performing at the Bolshoi Theatre in Burlington, Ontario. She has acted in numerous productions, such as the Tinker Bell and Penguin Christmas Drama at the Burlington Dance Academy Summer Camp.

Early Life

Douglas went to Burlington’s Nelson High School. During her travels before starting her acting career, Douglas studied a variety of subjects.

At the age of 6, she made her acting debut in F2: Forensic Factor. Douglas rose to fame in the fantasy TV series Spooksville as Sally Wilcox.

For her leading part in “Believe Me: The Kidnapping of Lisa McVeigh,” Douglas was nominated for an ACTRA award in 2019. Amy Beth McNulty, who played Anne and A, ultimately won the award.

Douglas portrayed Abby, Ginny’s friend and bandmate, in the Netflix series “Ginny and Georgia” in 2021. (Max, Abby, Nora, and Ginny).

Recognition & Nominations

For her performance as Sally Wilcox in Spooksville, Katie, then 15, was nominated for outstanding artist in a children’s series at the 41st Emmy Awards in Los Angeles in 2014.

Douglas’ portrayal of Lisa McVeigh in “Believe Me: The Abduction of Lisa McVeigh” earned her a nomination for Best Actress at the 17th Annual ACTRA Awards in Toronto.

Katie Douglas’s educational background includes a graduation from Nelson High School.
