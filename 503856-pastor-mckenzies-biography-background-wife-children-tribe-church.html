Controversies and arrest

Since launching his church in 2003, Pastor Paul has been labelled controversial. In addition, he has fallen in the arms of the law on several occasions, as follows:

2017

In 2017, the pastor and his wife appeared before Dr Julie Oseko, Malindi Chief Magistrate, where they were accused of promoting radicalization. However, the duo pleaded not guilty to the charges.

May 2019

Pastor Mckenzie was arraigned before Malindi Chief Magistrate for three charges, namely:

  • Disobedience of the law
  • Indoctrination of children
  • Religious incitement

In the first count, he was found in Malindi township on 4th April 2019, possessing cinematographic films aimed at inciting kids against attending school. The films also incited Christians against religions such as Buddhism, Hinduism and Islam.

In the second count, Paul was charged with possessing and distributing the film to the public before examination and classification by the Kenya Films Classification Board (KFCB). It was reported that between 1st January 2019 and 11th April 2019, he was found showcasing these films to the public at Good News International Church.
