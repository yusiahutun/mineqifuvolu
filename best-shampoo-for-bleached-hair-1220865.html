Choosing to do your hair your way is one of the best ways of self-expression. Be it for a ‘new hair, new you’ phase or just to have some fun with your look - there’s a multitude of reasons why you’d choose to bleach your hair. Regardless of why and where you decide to bleach your hair — in a professional salon or from the comfort of your home — ensuring that you maintain proper after-care is of utmost importance. That is precisely where we come in with our dedicated list of best shampoos for bleached hair. 

Professional hair stylist, and owner of Live Love Locks, Christopher Wenzel believes that there’s quite a knowledge gap about caring for blonde or bleached hair. He says, “The first thing to know when you’re going blonde, it’s going to cause some damage - that’s not a maybe, it’s a definitely. And, the darker your hair is, the more damage it is going to take to make it blonde. This process leads to holes in the outer layer of your hair, where the color is eliminated from.” He adds, “Your focus in terms of a routine should be on bringing back the moisture your hair loses and trying to steer away from the brittleness that’s caused by dry hair, once you do that it’s all about following it without too much of a hassle for you”.

    How to Choose the Best Shampoo for Bleached Hair?

    Sometimes when you bleach your hair, there are a lot of strong chemicals involved. This means that your hair is more or less bound to lose its natural shine and strength. The shampoos in our list above try their best to give your color-treated hair that hydration and nourishment back. Because as most cases go, caring for bleached hair starts at home, by setting up a routine that heals, protects, and prolongs the life of your color. Here are a few things to take into account when picking the shampoo for your newly bleached hair.  

    Understand the Problem
    Often, color-treated hair might act differently, especially when it has been a few weeks since your last salon visit. In such cases, it is important to take a closer look at how your hair in particular behaves. For some this might mean more brittleness or even split ends, for others, it could be yellowing tones, frizzy or rough hair. Once you understand your specific problem areas, it becomes easier to find a solution as most shampoos might directly focus on treating them. 

    Our contributor, Kate Ross, Hair and Beauty Specialist - ‘Irresistible Me’, shares a few tips to maintain the color of bleached hair without fading it out with frequent shampooing. 

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo formulated for color-treated hair.
  • Use dry shampoo in between washes
  • Rinse your hair with cool water to seal the cuticles
  • Use a purple shampoo once a week to neutralize brassiness
  • Apply a color-depositing conditioner or a gloss to refresh your tone.

    • Ingredients 

    As your hair has already been treated with a lot of chemicals, it would be a smart move to look for healthier options to maintain your colored hair. When choosing shampoos you can take a pick from a host of naturally derived ingredients ranging from coconut oil, aloe vera extracts, argan oil, jojoba oil, and even biotin or keratin infusions. Find the right combination of ingredients that work for you, the more organic the better.

    FAQ's

    Can I use any shampoo on my bleached hair?

    Well, the short answer is probably not. Your bleached hair and its shade are unique to you. Similarly, it is important to choose a shampoo that suits your hair and helps elevate it more rather than causing any further damage.

    What shampoo to avoid after bleaching hair?

    Considering that your hair has already been treated with a substantial amount of chemicals, it is smart to steer clear of shampoos that have more chemicals in them. Avoid those with high amounts of sulfates or sodium. It is best to choose a mild shampoo that gently cleanses without the chance of any damage.
