The Best Beret Styles - The Best Berets

March 2024 · 3 minute read

Che Guevara, Picasso, Bonnie Parker,  the fall runways of Gucci, Rodarte, Ralph Lauren and Stella McCartney, plus stereotypical Parisians everywhere—what do all of these have in common? The beret, of course. In this post-global warming fashion ecosystem, hats that actually promote warmth seem moot—while a beret perched insouciantly upon one's head makes nearly perfect sense. In the interest of science—or style—I asked BAZAAR editors to take berets to task to see if this look-of-the-moment is one they are willing to embrace on the streets. 

Kerry Pieri, Fashion/Features Director

Tyler Joe

"I am nearly always game for a quirky trend that embraces both Parisian and historical references, so I was eager to give the beret a spin. I opted for this vintage leather one that felt similar to the red one that walked on Gucci's Resort runway. However, because it was, in fact, leather, it kept slipping off of my head. While I like the idea of a beret in theory, in practice, it simply wasn't sensible.  Also, since I wore it with a mini skirt and turtleneck I think it was veering a little too female cadet."

Kerry's Verdict: I would try a beret in a more neutral hue and natural fabrication, worn with jeans and a knit for something less stylized. I'm not one to give up.

Vintage beanie, vestairecollective.com.

Lauren Fisher, Associate Editor

Tyler Joe

"I had hoped that putting a beret on would transform me into Brigitte Bardot but spoiler alert—it did not. Aside from the fact that it felt like it was going to fall off my head every 20 seconds and that I looked like I was ready to bake a baguette at a moment's notice, I don't really think I can pull off the whole beret look at all in real life."

Kerry's Verdict: Lauren favors midi skirts and minimal lines, so I think the beret fits perfectly into her current repertoire. I'd like the find her one with a better fit, but I thought this pom style looked perfect on her—especially for an afternoon at the café. 

Eugenia Kim beanie, eugeniakim.com.

Chrissy Rutherford, Senior Editor

Tyler Joe

"I've always thought berets were cute, and admired girls who wear them, but after trying on several for this shoot I'm pretty convinced that I'm not the kind of girl that can pull it off. It felt a little too costume-y—I'll stick to my beanies and fedoras."

Kerry's Verdict: It does seem like finding the right beret for you is an uphill battle, but because Chrissy does favor beanies I thought this look wasn't far off from that—just a little more charming, maybe? A girl in a beret really reinforces that whole "beauty is in the eye of the beholder" sentiment, because I still love them on other people.  

American Apparel beanie, americanapparel.net.

Kerry Pieri is Harper's Bazaar's Digital Fashion/Features Director.
