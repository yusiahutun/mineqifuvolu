Anthony Edwards slams Kevin Durant during Minnesota Timberwolves playoff win

August 2024 Â· 2 minute read

Kevin Durant might be one of the best players in NBA history and of the active NBA group, but even he couldn't stop the Minnesota Timberwolves juggernaut in the postseason and Anthony Edwards was quick to let the Pheonix Suns' star know.

The first game of the series finished 120-95 in favor of Edwards' team. Edwards himself, starred with 33 points and a critical three-pointer at the Target Center, before he directed his attention to his rival for some good ole trash talk.

The Timberwolves' number five, aged 22, however, was full of praise for Durant afterwards and lauded on the praise and admitted he simply got carried away in the moment during the April 20 contest.

"I think everyone here knows that's my favorite player of all time," Edwards said. "So that was probably one of the best feelings ever in my whole life."

How did Durant react? The NBA great took it all in his stride and laughed it off, showing excellent sportsmanship despite the heavy loss he received in Suns' purple.

"It's just basketball,'' Durant said. "Not even playoffs, it's just hoop.

"You get hot, you make shots, you make tough shots, you're gonna feel excited about yourself. So it's on me to keep coming back."

Edwards jaw-dropped by Durant

Despite Edwards' stand out display, even he was caught up in the moment of Durant's legacy and talent as the veteran nailed shot after shot after shot at one stage, so the youngster praised him after the game.

The pair will next face off on Tuesday, April 24 at the Target Center once again so it will be interesting to see what they will say after round two.

"Did you see him in the third quarter?" Edwards said of Durant. "I felt like we were supposed to be up 15, 18 in the third quarter early. And if I'm not mistaken, he made four or five straight buckets like it was nothing.

"And I became a fan at one point. I was out there like, '[Damn], he's nice.' There's nothing we can do... I'm looking at the stat sheet, he's 11-for-17 [from the field].

"He missed six shots and had 31. I mean, he's the greatest to ever do it, man. Big tip of my hat to him, he's the best."
