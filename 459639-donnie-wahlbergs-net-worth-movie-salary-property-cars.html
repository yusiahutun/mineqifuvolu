Career beginnings

The budding career of Donnie Wahlberg as an entertainer was birthed when he was in high school. Wahlberg and his friend Danny Wood were in Copley High School when they formed a rap group called the Kool-Aid Bun.

He was 15 when he auditioned for music producer Maurice Starr. Maurice was on a mission to create America’s first big boy band and liked Donnie’s singing, rapping, and dancing skills, and immediately enlisted him.

Rise to fame

After being enlisted, Donnie brought his younger brother Mark and friend Danny on board. Jordan Knight and his older brother Jonathan were also recruited. Mark did not like the band’s artistic direction and left soon after.

New Kids on the Block signed with Columbia Records, and their first album came out in 1986. The album performed dismally, but their second album gave them a slow start to success. Please Don’t Go Girl was the best single on the album, and it reached number 10 on the Billboard charts. By 1989, their second album had managed to become the No. 1 album in the US.

During the peak of the band’s career, NKOTB reached tens of millions of record sales and more than $400 million worth of merchandise. The group's popularity started fading in the early 90s prompting Donnie to focus on helping Mark launch his own music career. Donnie co-wrote and produced Mark’s first platinum-certified album.
