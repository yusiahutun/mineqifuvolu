What to Keep in Mind

Reusable or Not

Nipple covers come in both disposable and reusable options. Deciding which to buy all comes down to personal preference and lifestyle, though there are some pluses to both alternatives. Since reusable nipple covers are worn more than once, they are more environmentally friendly, more cost-effective, and often made with more skin-friendly materials to provide comfort and flexibility with each wear. Disposable pasties are more convenient since they are designed for single use, are more travel friendly, and eliminate the need for washing since you’ll be sporting a fresh cover every time.

Material

Nipple pasties are made of soft, skin-friendly materials like silicone, fabric, or adhesive paper. Silicone — possibly the most popular option — is soft, flexible, and durable, while other covers, particularly disposable, are made from smooth fabrics like satin, nylon, and cotton. Choosing a material comes down to personal preference, but it’s essential to follow the tag’s instructions for proper application, care, and usage. 

Shape

If you can think of a shape, chances are, there’s a nipple cover made in its image. Seriously, though — there are petal-shaped pasties and heart-shaped covers; stars, crosses, butterflies, you name it. However, the most popular and classic option is a circular-shaped nipple cover because it is simple and provides optimal coverage.

Your Questions, Answered

How do nipple covers stay on?

Nipple covers (AKA nipple pasties or nipple petals) include an adhesive backing designed to conceal the nipple while wearing clothing. They include a sticky, medical-grade adhesive on one side to ensure the cover stays in place and naturally conforms to the breast. 

How do you take nipple covers off?

Each nipple cover application should include skin prep and removal, like your face makeup routine. For removal, gently and slowly peel off the nipple covers from the outside edge toward the center. In some cases where the adhesive is too stubborn to remove, an oil-based product like coconut oil, baby oil, or bra tape-specific removers will loosen the stick. A damp cloth or warm water may help, too. Avoid pulling on the skin directly, especially if the adhesive is still sticking, as this can cause discomfort and irritation to tender parts of the chest. 

What size should nipple covers be?

Nipple covers come in an extensive range of sizes and shapes to accommodate plenty of breast sizes. To determine the appropriate size, you can measure the diameter of the nipple (the distance across the circular part), as most are labeled with diameter size (i.g., 3 inches, 3.5 inches). This is all, of course, dependent on coverage preference and style choice — if you want something with a little less coverage, we say go for it! Just remember to size down.
