Early life

Khalid Al Ameri was born on December 5, 1983, in Abu Dhabi, United Arabs Emirates. He is of mixed ethnicity, born to an Emirati (Unite Arabs Emirates national) father and a Scottish father. His parents met in Falkirk, Scotland, where his father was studying.

They got married in 1982 and relocated to Abu Dhabi, where his father worked as an accountant and his mother as a nurse. Khalid was the firstborn, and he has two younger brothers. He is close with his family, including his father and mother, who regularly feature in his YouTube and Facebook videos.

Khalid spent his childhood in Abu Dhabi with his parents and brothers. He had a great childhood with loving parents. However, he has also expressed that he struggled to fit in due to his mixed ethnicity. Over the years, though, he learned to embrace his identity, especially his Emirati ethnicity. Khalid attended the American International School in Abu Dhabi, where he completed his elementary and secondary school education. After graduating, he moved to the United Kingdom, where he attended Northumbria University in Newcastle. At the institution, he studied Maritime Operations, graduating with a B.S. degree in 2006.

After graduating, he returned to the United Arab Emirates, where he entered the workforce. However, he would travel abroad several years later for more studying, this time to the U.S.A. He attended Stanford University in 2012, pursuing a Master of Business Administration, graduating in 2014. While in the U.S., he developed an interest in media, which would lead him down the path of digital content creation.

Career

After returning from the U.K. for his studies, Khalid worked at the Mubadala Development Company, an investment management company in Abu Dhabi. He stayed with the company for nearly nine years, rising to the position of senior associate. He also worked in several capacities in U.A.E., such as a manager at a Learning & Development clinic in Abu Dhabi and a mentor at Flat6Labs (a digital innovation hub).

After returning from the U.S.A. for his M.B.A. studies, he switched careers and started focusing on the media industry. He started working as a columnist for The Gulf Today, a U.A.E. daily newspaper covering issues about youth, education, social development, and entrepreneurship. He was also a contributor to The National, another U.A.E. newspaper.

In May 2016, he landed a job with CNN, where he worked as a presenter for the Ramadhan Series, a weekly program. Sadly, the job only lasted for one month, leaving him to search for something else. At this point, he started creating content and sharing it online at his wife's encouragement.

Today, Khalid is one of the biggest social media influencers in Abu Dhabi. He has nearly eight million followers on Facebook, where he regularly posts videos. He also has a massive following on YouTube (1.67 million subscribers), Instagram (2.2 million followers) and Tik Tok (4.8 million followers).
