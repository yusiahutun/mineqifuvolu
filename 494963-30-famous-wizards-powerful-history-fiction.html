30 most famous wizards who are powerful in history and fiction

May 2024 · 13 minute read

If you enjoy epic fantasy movies with magic and mysticism, you've probably encountered some powerful wizards. From Merlin in King Arthur to Dumbledore in the Harry Potter franchise, the genre has had its fair share of wizards. However, magic doesn't only exist in fiction because history has recorded tales of some famous wizards. Regardless of whether or not they lived, each wizard has their strengths and rise to fame. As a result, fans are naturally curious about who they are.

Famous wizards from history and fiction have a large following. Their abilities have elevated them above their peers, some protagonists and antagonists. If you enjoy magic or fan fiction, these magicians may interest you. However, who ranks first may be a matter of personal preference.

30 famous wizards in history

There have been many famous wizards throughout history. Some are ancient legends, while others are from fiction, film, books and television shows. Regardless of their origin, one thing is for sure; they are powerful. Their strength and accomplishments have made them famous. So, if you're looking for famous wizards throughout history, the ones below are some of the most notable.

1. Alastor Moody – Harry Potter series

Alastor "Mad-Eye" Moody is one of the most famous wizards in movies, thanks to his abilities and skills. He was a pure-blood wizard, an Auror, and a member of the Order of the Phoenix.

He was known for being neurotic and possessing a supernatural eye that allowed him to see through everything. When the Second Wizarding War began, Mad-Eye returned to the order to battle Lord Voldemort and his Death Eaters but died during the Battle of the Seven Potters.

2. Albus Dumbledore – Harry Porter series

Who is the most famous wizard? Albus Dumbledore takes the spot as the most famous wizard thanks to his extraordinary magical powers and abilities. He is the headmaster of the wizarding school Hogwarts.

Dumbledore is among the good and powerful wizards. He is benevolent and wise and exudes a sense of calm and serenity, rarely displaying emotions such as anger or fear.

5. Doctor Victor Von Doom – Marvel Cinematic Universe

Doctor Victor Von Doom is a Marvel Comics supervillain. He is a politician who serves as the Kingdom of Latveria's Monarch and Supreme Leader. Dr Doom was scarred in an accident and now hides his true face behind an iron mask and armour.

He employs his scientific abilities to steal or replicate the powers of other beings. In addition to being a brilliant scientist and inventor, Doom is also a mighty sorcerer.

6. Emperor Palpatine – Star Wars franchise

Sheev Palpatine, also known as Darth Sidious in the Star Wars universe, is the Supreme Chancellor of the Galactic Republic and Emperor of the Galactic Empire. Darth Plagueis taught him the dark side of the Force.

He employs the Sith Alchemy and Magick Force that Talzin taught him. Sith magic, also known as Sith sorcery, was an arcane expression of Force ability developed and practised by the original Sith species to manipulate the dark side's power.

7. Gandalf – The Lord of the Rings, The Hobbit

Gandalf is a powerful wizard in The Hobbit and The Lord of the Rings. He is a member of the Istari order and the Fellowship of the Ring leader. Gandalf's magical power is in light and fire, which he can manipulate by casting light beams, starting and directing fires, and more. Although he possesses tremendous magical power, he has limitations.

8. Medea – Greek mythology

According to Greek mythology, Medea is the daughter of King Aetes of Colchis, the niece of Circe, and the granddaughter of the sun god Helios. She is an enchantress who assisted Jason, the leader of the Argonauts, in obtaining the Golden Fleece from her father, King Aetes of Colchis. Medea is of divine descent and possesses the gift of prophecy.

9. Glinda the Good Witch – The Wizard of Oz

Glinda is a fictional character from the Oz novels. She is the most powerful sorceress in Oz, ruler of the Quadling Country south of the Emerald City, and Princess Ozma's protector. Glinda is the sister of the other three witches of Oz, Addaperle (Abrakadabra), Evillene (Sadista), and Evvamean.

Her abilities included Telekinesis, which allowed her to levitate objects without touching them with her wand. Glinda could also fly and avoid danger by using her wand.

10. Godric Gryffindor – Harry Potter series

Godric Gryffindor was an English wizard and one of the four founders of Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. He was said to be the greatest duellist of his time, with considerable talent in martial magic.

Godric was an expert at charm work, assisting in enchanting the Sorting Hat into a sentient magical artefact with personality. He could also create a non-corporeal Patronus, demonstrating his superior magical abilities as a wizard.

11. Harry Dresden – The Dresden Files series

Harry "Blackstone Copperfield" Dresden is a human wizard in the fiction series the Dresden Files. He is a powerful wizard who has mastered thaumaturgy, alchemy, and necromancy arts. Dresden uses wind, fire, and ice as his primary source of weapons.

12. Harry Porter – Harry Potter series

Who is the coolest wizard? Harry Potter is among the coolest wizard in the magical world. Among his feats is surviving an attack by Lord Voldemort when he was a baby. Harry is the only child of James and Lily Potter and is also known as 'The boy who lived'.

Harry's abilities include native power that allows him to perform advanced magic at a younger age than other wizards. He also has the most remarkable capacity for love, which causes Voldemort intense physical pain whenever he enters his mind.

16. Lord Voldemort – Harry Potter series

Lord Voldemort is one of the most potent evil wizards in the magical world. He is a dark wizard seeking unrivalled power and immortality through the practice of the Dark Arts.

Merlin is a mythical figure who appears prominently in the legend of King Arthur and is best known as a magician. Because of his abilities, he is one of the world's most famous male wizards.

According to legend, Merlin could battle gods alone, teleport anywhere in the world, and summon mythological creatures to do his bidding. He also possessed astral projection, Telekinesis, reality manipulation, and the ability to bring anyone back to life.

18. Morgana le Fay – Legend of King Arthur

Morgana le Fay was the ruler of the magical island of Avalon and is among the famous wizards in mythology. She is a powerful and ambiguous enchantress from the legend of King Arthur. In many stories, Morgan is the arch-enemy of both Merlin and Arthur.

Her origin, based on her name, is some fairy creature. She possesses magical powers that allow her to wreak havoc across the land. Morgana has remained a powerful sorceress, even though her exact nature and role differ from one story to the next.

19. Newt Scamander – Fantastic Beasts

Newton Artemis Fido "Newt" Scamander is a Magizoologist wizard who wrote Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them, a book used in the wizarding world, especially Hogwarts. Newt is famous for his care of magical creatures, knowledge of all types of animals, and how to communicate with them.

20. Professor Severus Snape – Harry Porter series

Professor Severus Snape is a highly skilled wizard with a cold and resentful exterior that conceals deep emotions and anguish. He is among the famous wizards in Harry Potter thanks to his ability to concoct complex potions like Wolfsbane Potion and Veritaserum.

Snape can also cast nonverbal spells and perform wandless magic. In addition, he is also a dark magic genius and a skilled Legilimens (the ability to read minds).

21. Prospero – The Tempest

Prospero is a sorcerer and the rightful Duke of Milan in Shakespeare's play The Tempest. He lives on an enchanted island with his daughter, Miranda, where he acts as a god, manipulating everyone within his grasp. However, he is helpless against his enemies until they appear on a nearby ship, where he can use his magic to create a storm and subdue them.

22. Credence Barebone – Fantastic Beasts film series

Credence Barebone, born Aurelius Dumbledore, is a 20th-century English-born American half-blood wizard. His parents are Aberforth Dumbledore and an unnamed woman. Credence's magical abilities and skills include being an Obscurus, a human host for an uncontrollable supernatural force known as an Obscurus, which forms when wizards attempt to suppress their magical abilities.

23. Raistlin Majere – Dragonlance series

Raistlin Majere is the anti-hero turned villain from the Dragonlance series of books. He is a Wizard of High Sorcery who first wears the red robes of a Lunitari worshipper. Raistlin has golden skin that protects him from magical attacks. He also has hourglass eyes from a curse given by Par-Salian, the head of the Mages Conclave, on Krynn.

24. Saruman the White – Lord of the Rings

Saruman is the first of the Istari (Wizards), Valar emissaries sent to Middle-earth in the Third Age to aid in the defeat of the resurrected Sauron. He wished to share in the Dark Lord's power as a quasi-equal lieutenant by his side or by claiming the One ring first, thus betraying his order.

Saruman was a powerful wizard who managed to imprison Gandalf the Grey. He is well-versed in Ringlore and mechanics and can bend even the most influential minds to his will.

25. Sauron – The Lord of The Rings

Sauron was a highly gifted Maia, a primordial spirit created to assist the Valar in the world's creation. He was Aul's student and learned to craft and make things.

Sauron's power stems from his ability to corrupt others. He creates a ring to manipulate others but fails because his strength lies in the ring, which diminishes after Bilbo Baggins destroys it at Mount Doom.

26. Shazam the Wizard – Marvel Universe

Shazam, the wizard, was an ancient sorcerer who bestowed the superhero Shazam with his name and powers. He was an ancient Egyptian who fought evil for centuries in his Golden Age incarnation.

When he becomes too old to continue, he transfers some of his power to a young teen, Billy, who transforms into a superhero when he mentions his name.

Although the wizard Shazam has passed away, the teen, Billy, can summon his ghost for brief periods and continues to guide him.

27. The Enchanters Three – Marvel Universe

The Enchanters are brothers from the realm of Ringsfjord, which exists in the Marvel Cinematic Universe on the extradimensional continent of Asgard. They are Forsung, Brona, and Magnir, and the Living Talismans they possess increase their power.

The Enchanters have varying degrees of magical power, including immunity to ageing and disease. They can fire energy beams, manipulate inanimate matter, mutate and control humans, levitate large masses of Earth into the air and travel between multiple dimensions.

28. The White Witch – The Chronicles of Narnia

Queen Jadis, the White Witch, is the main antagonist of The Chronicles of Narnia series. She is ruthless, cruel, power-hungry, and sadistic, claiming the throne of Narnia by brute Force and enchanting the land, so it is always winter.

29. The Wizard of Oz – The Wizard of Oz

The Wizard of Oz was a powerful witch and the dark side of Clive Dillon (a member of the men of letters) who wanted to rule over Oz. When Clive discovered the key to Oz, he travelled there, leaving the key on Earth and becoming trapped in Oz.

Clive was kidnapped in Oz by a coven of evil witches who used the Inner Key of Oz to split him into his good and dark sides, resulting in the creation of the Wizard of Oz from Clive's dark side. The wizard was evil and power-hungry, slaughtering the coven and vying for control of Oz. Clive eventually gets rescued, but the evil wizard remains in Oz.

30. Aleister Crowley

Aleister Crowley is among the most famous and controversial male wizards in history. He was an English occultist, philosopher, ceremonial magician, poet, painter, novelist, and mountaineer. Crowley established the religion of Thelema, identifying himself as the prophet. He was allegedly able to spiritually knock people to the ground using imitative magic without any preparation.
