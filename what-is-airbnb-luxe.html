Airbnb Launches Luxe Rentals The World's Best Luxury Homes

March 2024 · 6 minute read

What began as Brian Chesky and Joe Gebbia setting up air mattresses in their San Francisco living room 11 years ago has since skyrocketed into a travel phenomenon. Now, when deciding where to rest one's head when traveling both domestically and abroad, travelers ask themselves "hotel or home rental?" before all else. And let's be honest: nobody actually says "home rental." Because the experience of booking and staying in a home or private room when you're away from your own has become synonymous with one brand: Airbnb. The brand's name is both a company and a noun; it's even a verb, like when you Airbnb your apartment when you're out of town.

After years of steady success, and a magazine published by Hearst, the brand's invention of and monopoly on the home rental space led to the acquisition of Luxury Retreats in 2017, allowing Chesky and Gebbia to go toe-to-toe with sites offering a higher end rental product, like VRBO, owned by AirBnb's direct competitor, HomeAway.

Their desire to take a step further in catering to a luxury client base stems, of course, from lots of data. A representative of the brand noted that in 2018, the number of Airbnb bookings priced at least $1,000 per night increased by more than 60 percent. With a rise in consumers craving all things super-luxe, Airbnb decided to go all-in on the top 10 percent. Enter Airbnb Luxe.

Airbnb announced the launch of Luxe on June 25, their highest-end offering yet. The brand has previously offered Airbnb Plus, where homes—that averaged at an accessible $150/night—were further vetted for "guests looking for beautiful homes, exceptional hosts and added peace of mind knowing homes are quality inspected and verified in person for cleanliness, comfort and design," a representative for AirBnb told BAZAAR.com.

Airbnb Luxe is intended for only the world's most extraordinary homes, catering to those willing to spend upwards of $1,500/night for a (literal) home away from home. Via the Airbnb app, travelers can now choose chateaux, villas, townhouses, penthouses and more across the globe, some housing upwards of 20+ guests. Loyalists to Luxury Retreats will notice some crossover in the Airbnb Luxe and Luxury Retreats offerings, but the brand ensures that homes listed on Luxe are curated to resonate with the Airbnb audience. To be listed, Luxe homes must pass a "strict evaluation across 300+ criteria to meet standards in both design and function."

Airbnb confirms that currently, "Luxe listings are all pulled from the Luxury Retreats portfolio, though the service offerings differ if you booked with Airbnb vs. Luxury Retreats on a given home. The [entire] Luxury Retreats portfolio doesn’t necessarily align with the Luxe home standards, and went through an added layer of criteria to be selected." In short, Airbnb Luxe has just taken high-end rentals up a notch.

Courtesy of Airbnb Courtesy of Airbnb

To put the criteria necessary for being listed on Luxe into perspective: The Chateau in Provence featured in the latest episodes of The Real Housewives of Beverly Hills, Chateau de Tourreau, is listed on Airbnb. Joe Jonas and Sophie Turner are also rumored to be getting married there this weekend. That property, however, is not listed on Airbnb Luxe, despite its rate of $4,744/night. Properties featured on the new Luxe platform include The Fleming Villa in Jamaica, where Ian Fleming penned his famous novels; Nukutepipi, curated by Guy Laliberte, founder of Cirque du Soleil and Lune Rouge, in French Polynesia; a sleek and chic penthouse in London's Sloane Square, a 10-bedroom chateau in France; and an otherworldly modern getaway in New Zealand.

In case a past Airbnb rental experience (be it private or shared) still has you in doubt, BAZAAR.com asked the brand about its methods of evaluation for Luxe rental properties. "Airbnb’s local Host Team sources homes and uses our design standard as a benchmark for initial evaluation," a representative from the brand confirms. "They then score properties against our design rubric to ensure that all aspects of the exterior design, interior amenities, and design elements are in like-new condition."

AirBnb also vets the property’s technology, security, privacy, and overall accessibility. Recommended homes are then re-evaluated by a Global Design Expert, who scores the home. Only then is the property sent into the on-boarding process (including an in-person visit from a Home Consultant with a 300-point checklist in hand).

The main difference between a standard and a Luxe rental though, is a guest's around-the-clock access to AirBnb's trip design team who "ensure the stay is tailored to the traveler’s unique needs every step of the way." Think of it as Luxe's answer to the hotel butler or concierge.

"After booking a property, the trip designer can help ensure effortless check-in, coordinating local bespoke experiences and activities, and arranging a wide range of services from childcare to private chefs to in-house massage therapists—or, for the dedicated, personal training sessions in your own private gym," explains AirBnb.

These trip designers, along with the site's in-depth criteria for Luxe properties, are intended to guarantee a seamless and otherworldly experience, without the need for a hotel stay.

However, like anyone on a hotel staff, the team at Airbnb Luxe and its trip designers aren't magicians. "The speed of resolution greatly depends on the particular issue," the brand's representative shared with BAZAAR.com. "The trip design team is equipped with emergency 24/7 contacts in each destination, so there’s always someone 'on the ground' to help solve any issues."

And while most luxury initiatives are anchored in exclusivity, the goal of Airbnb Luxe appears to be inclusivity. Most luxury brands create diffusion offerings after launching an upscale product, aiming to democratize their services after catering solely to the 1 percent. Airbnb has catered to budgets small and large since its launch 11 years ago, and added Airbnb Plus to tap into the growing desire for convenience and quality, rather than just a solution to a common travel problem. Luxe is their answer to the customer looking for true luxury: curation, connection, value, and above all, service. “The launch of Airbnb Luxe is also part of our continued effort to ensure Airbnb is for everyone," the brand said in their launch announcement.

Brian Chesky, Airbnb Co-Founder, CEO and Head of Community added, "Today’s luxury traveler is craving more than just high-end accommodations; they seek transformation and experiences that leave them feeling more connected to each other and to their destination. With Airbnb Luxe, we are applying the same approach we’ve used since we launched Airbnb more than 11 years ago—creating local, authentic, and magical travel moments now in amazing places to stay—to reimagine the way people think and experience luxury travel.”
