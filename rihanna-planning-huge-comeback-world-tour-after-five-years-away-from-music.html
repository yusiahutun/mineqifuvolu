Rihanna 'planning huge comeback world tour' after five years away from music

March 2024 · 3 minute read

It’s the moment the Navy has been waiting for for years, Rihanna is reportedly making a comeback to music after a five-year hiatus from music, with a huge tour.

The music star first came onto the scene in 2005, when she awed audiences with her first hit single, Pon De Replay. She went on to achieve countless achievements in music, including an astonishing 9 Grammys. However, she’s taken a break from the tunes for a while, focusing on her makeup line, Fenty Beauty, and her two sons, RZA and Riot. However, it looks like fans may not have to wait much longer…

Rihanna reportedly planning comeback to music with world tour

The Umbrella singer is said to be planning a huge £32 million ($38.3 million) comeback world tour for 2024/2025 the Mirror reports.

As well as this, the publication reports is also said to ” have two albums worth of new material to release that she has been working on.”

A source tells them: “Rihanna is quietly planning a comeback tour after signing with Live Nation. The deal was created to facilitate a world tour and her creative team is quietly at work in Los Angeles putting it all together as she raises her family.”

Recently, the mom of two announced the arrival of her second son, Riot Rose, with boyfriend A$AP Rocky, with adorable family pics.

However, those wanting to mark the date in their calendar for ticket sales may have to wait a while, as the source tells the Mirror that she’ll press play “once she’s ready to get back.”

Although the star has had a five-year hiatus from the scene, her last track, Lift Me Up, was released in 2022 for the movie Black Panther: Wakanda Forever. Even after so long, the 35-year-old received an Oscar nomination for the song, for Best Original Song.

Her last big public performance came at the 2023 Superbowl Half-Time Show, where she iconically announced her second pregnancy after performing a melody of her hits, revealing her baby bump to the world.

However, her last world tour was her Anti World Tour, back in 2016.

A replica of the star’s Superbowl jumpsuit sells out

Speaking of her iconic Superbowl performance, watched by 121 million fans, Loewe recently dropped a “special edition release” of a red cargo jumpsuit inspired by her performance earlier this year.

The pieces were going for $2,900, and all sold out within 24 hours, and we’re not surprised.

Hopefully, the star will bring more iconic outfits for inspo on her reported upcoming tour, although some fans on X, formerly known as Twitter, think it’s too good to be true.

“Rihanna, reading this post: ‘When did I agree to this?'” joked one.

Another said: “Does Rihanna know this?” questioned another.

However, others can’t hold in their excitement: “Rihanna can take my whole check bc baby I will be in the front row and nothing short of that.”
