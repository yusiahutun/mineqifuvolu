13 Flattering Mom Jeans Outfits and How to Wear Them

June 2024 · 4 minute read

While fashion trends always seem to be in a state of rotation, we know this much to be true: Baggy pants are in, and that's especially the case with denim. However, if you're someone who usually gravitates toward slim-fitting bottoms, it's possible you've wondered how to style a mom jeans outfit. What shoes do you wear? Will it look too dad-like with a button-down? After all, the goal is to find a combination that feels fresh and fun — and not like the punchline of a Saturday Night Live skit.

The good news is these pants are incredibly versatile, and the newer versions are way more flattering than their predecessors (think: stretchy material that hugs all the right places, subtle distressed accents, and various fits). The main detail that all styles have in common is a high rise, so thoughtful styling is key. Consider whether you prefer a style with a fully wide leg, a slightly tapered leg, a cropped length, or a looser (i.e. baggy) fit through the hips and thighs, and adjust the rest of your ensemble accordingly.

To help you navigate the options, here are our favorite mom jeans outfit ideas.

Try a Tucked-In Sweater

Give your mom jeans a dose of laid-back luxe with a thick sweater tucked in at the front. Complete the look with a gorgeous oversized coat, white sneakers, classic sunnies, and a structured bag.

Roll Your Cuffs

Breathe new life into your mom jeans with a rolled cuff that exposes the ankles, leaving you with ample room to explore complementary footwear like heels and gym socks, Chelsea boots, or high top sneakers.

Break Out Your Best Blouse

One of the hallmarks of the mom jean is a high waist. Tucking in a blouse or button-down is an easy way to elevate the cut and look polished. Consider a white pair and a skinny belt paired with delicate heels or sandals, and you've got an office or casual lunch-ready look.

Try a Crop Top

Take advantage of the style's high rise and go for a crop top. How much midriff you reveal is up to you, but you can go casual with a muscle tee and sneakers or dress them up with a collared shirt and stilettos or strappy sandals for a nighttime look.

Choose Edgy Accessories

How you accessorize your mom jeans is key to updating them season after season, so look for edgy complements like pointed flats with heavy metallic detailing, a cool phone crossbody, or an of-the-moment belt.

Get Playful

Consider your mom jeans the blank canvas and your accessories the paint. A pair of colorful Jordan kicks or another statement sneaker and a textured coat are a great way to adorn this cut, and a baseball cap or hat also give the look an intentional and playful feel.

Get Comfy

Fully lean into the comfortable nature of mom jeans with a hoodie and, of course, dad sneakers. Really push the look into stylish but oh-so-cozy territory with an open wool or trench coat and knit cap or beanie during the colder months.

Consider Your Socks

If you're stumped by what to do with a cropped or shorter pair of mom jeans, embrace the cut by developing a strong sock game. Pair your socks (crisp white or patterned) with a pair of loafers, sneakers, or even lug sole boots.

Choose a Bodysuit

Keep your silhouette close to the body is a subtle way of wearing bodycon clothing without having to reveal too much (or anything!). Complement your bodysuit look with a cropped jacket and rolled cuffs that expose a cute pair of boots.

Bust Out a Pair of Converse

The classic Converse sneaker works with almost any look, but mom jeans really get a boost from their longevity, regardless of the cut. Consider pairing your cropped mom jeans with a pair of high tops.

Tuck Your Jeans Into Boots

Love your mom jeans but want to show off a new pair of boots? No problem! Roll and tuck your mom jeans into your favorite heeled boots for a more versatile look.

Embrace the '90s

Mom jeans fit perfectly into the resurgence of all things '90s. Try styling yours with chunky-soled boots or loafers, a big belt, and a halter top or racerback tee for maximum impact.

Go Full-On Preppy

Preppy always looks polished, so give your mom jeans an upper-crust European twist with a blazer, blouse, penny loafers, and a sweater casually draped over the shoulders. Add a modern twist with a baseball cap in a fun fabric like tweed or leather.
