    How to Choose a Neutral Eyeshadow Palette?

    Muted shades like creams, beiges, taupes, browns, dusty pinks, grays, and a few more encapsulate the neutral eyeshadow palette. These shades are suitable for all skin tones and deliver infinite possibilities for eye makeup looks. Our list is a line-up of the best palettes available in the market, but before you take the leap and pick your favorite, here are certain things to bear in mind. 

    • Neutrals primarily lie between cool and warm tones, however, each palette serves a mix of varying undertones and finishes. Look for the ones that best match your skin complexion and enhance your overall look. 
    • Whether you are adding a new palette to your AM makeup routine or need a professional range of colors, the variety of shades matters the most. From compact four-pan palettes to 16 shadow panels, there are many options to choose from. A mini and travel-friendly palette is great for everyday looks, while a wide-ranging pan is ideal for creating detailed, glam looks. 
    • Another factor to consider is the texture and finish. If your taste gravitates towards subtlety, stick to mattes only. For adding some glitz and drama, opt for palettes that have a mix of mattes and shimmery metallic eyeshadows. 
    • For ease of application, ensure that the shadows are highly pigmented, give off a true wash of color, last for a long, and go on smoothly. A creamy-to-powder formula works best for every makeup lover. 
    • Lastly, if you are someone with sensitive skin or eyes, ensure that the product formulation is hypoallergic, gluten-free, oil-free, paraben-free, and free from agents like talc.

    How to Use a Neutral Eyeshadow Palette?

    Here are the steps for perfect neutral eyeshadow application:

    Step 1: Begin by prepping your eyelids with an eyeshadow primer or concealer to create a buildable base.

    Step 2: For a minimalist look, you can swipe your lids with one or a couple of shades, like beiges and muted browns, using your fingertips or brush. 

    Step 3: Coming on to some added drama and gilded glam, enhance your looks with smokey, chromatic, or shimmery eye makeup by blending two or more shades.

    Step 4: An eyeshadow brush is essential for blendability and color deposit on the lids. Begin blending the applied eyeshadow well.

    Step 5: You can draw a bold stroke of eyeliner to complete your look and add much-needed definition to your eyes. 

    Quick Tip: 

    To avoid staining your makeup base with shadow fall-out, It’s advisable to begin your makeup with the eyes first. After finishing up the eye makeup, clean the face and then start with your full-face base makeup.
