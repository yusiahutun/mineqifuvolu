Elena Rendell: charged with murdering her younger sister after shooting her during a fight over a cell phone

A 17-year-old girl has been accused of killing her 14-year-old sister in an argument over a cell phone.

Elena Rendell, 17, is alleged to have shot her younger sister Christina, 14, in the neck as they fought over the phone at their Florida home.

Investigators said Elena had grabbed a 9mm pistol from a bedroom and pointed it at her sister, firing a single shot.

Neighbours said she ran from the house after the shooting, shouting for help and saying her sister had been shot.

Paramedics took Christina to hospital, but they were unable to save her.

Elena was arrested at the scene in Pensacola, Florida, and taken to a juvenile detention center on aggravated battery charges.

She was due to appear before a judge when officials broke the news to her that her sister had died from her injuries.

While her parents and other family members sobbed loudly in the public seating area, Elena stared straight ahead showing no emotion.

Elena occasionally looked round at her family as the charges were upgraded to second degree murder.

Officials said she was charged as an adult as a firearm was involved. The maximum sentence is life imprisonment.

The teen is reported to have told her lawyer that the shooting was an accident. She said if she had run away it would have looked like a crime.

The Escambia County Sheriff's Office reported the girls to be foster sisters.

'It appears that the two juveniles were at home and involved in some sort of argument,' said sheriff’s office spokesman Deputy Chris Welborn.

At some point, Rendell retrieved a firearm and shot the victim.

Emotions ran high when Elena appeared before the juvenile court with family members taking up the entire back row of the courtroom.

One of the family members, a middle-age man, cradled his head in his hands and sobbed quietly. Another clutched a Bible.

Assistant State Attorney John Molchan said: 'At this point, they're crushed. It's certainly a horrific tragedy.'

Elena's soldier brother pleaded with the judge to release the girl into his care.

Army Sgt. Jason Rendell told the judge: 'She listens to me. She respects me. I can deal with her'.

Judge Ross Goodman rejected the plea and Elena was held on $300,000 bond.
