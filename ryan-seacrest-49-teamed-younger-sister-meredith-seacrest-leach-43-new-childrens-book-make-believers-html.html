Ryan Seacrest is embarking on a new project with help from a family member.

The American Idol host, 49, and his younger sister, 43-year-old Meredith Seacrest Leach, have combined their talents to pen the children's book The Make-Believers.

'We’re excited to share that two of the most imaginative people we know, Ryan Seacrest and Meredith Seacrest Leach, are tag-teaming another item on their bucket list: Authors ✅' the Ryan Seacrest Foundation announced Tuesday.

'Their new children’s book, The Make-Believers, will hit shelves this fall!'

They posed for a new photo together to promote the book. 

Ryan Seacrest, 49, and his younger sister, Meredith Seacrest Lynch, 43, have combined their talents to write a new children's book

The story was inspired by 'their upbringing, their careers, and their personal adventures,' the group shared, revealing, 'the goal of this story is to encourage kids to dream big, imagine an extraordinary life, and in turn, create a world that is entirely their own.' 

In an interview with People, the pair explained during their childhood in Atlanta they both 'wondered what life was like beyond our everyday surroundings.

'From a young age, we discovered that our imaginations doubled as tools to dream big and travel far.'

Now they want to encourage young readers to look beyond their own circumstances for something more. 

'We used our imaginations to envision an extraordinary life, like the ones we saw in the movies and television or in magazines and books,' they told the publication. 

The siblings have already realized their dream of helping others through the Ryan Seacrest foundation, which the family established in 2009.

Meredith is the executive director of the non-profit organization which is 'dedicated to inspiring today’s youth through entertainment and education focused initiatives.'

The group's primary initiative is to build broadcast media centers, named Seacrest Studios, within pediatric hospitals so that young patients can explore the creative realms of radio, television and new media.

So far, they have built 14 Seacrest Studios across the US.

'We want to help kids realize their dreams and believe in a world that is entirely their own.'
