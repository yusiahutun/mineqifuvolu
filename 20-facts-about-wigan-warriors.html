A Rich History

Established in 1872, the Wigan Warriors are one of the oldest and most successful rugby league teams in the world.

Home at the DW Stadium

The Wigan Warriors play their home matches at the iconic DW Stadium, which they share with the Wigan Athletic Football Club.

Record-Breaking Titles

The Warriors have an impressive record of winning the most Rugby Football League championships, with a staggering total of 22 titles to their name.

Grand Slam Triumphs

The team has achieved the feat of winning all four major domestic trophies in a single season on three occasions: 1991-92, 1992-93, and 1994-95.

Strong Rivalry with St. Helens

The rivalry between the Wigan Warriors and the St. Helens Rugby Football Club is one of the most intense and long-standing in British Rugby League.

A History of Great Players

Over the years, the Wigan Warriors have produced some of the sport’s greatest players, including Andy Farrell, Shaun Edwards, and Ellery Hanley.

Coaching Legacy

The team has been fortunate to have had renowned coaches like Shaun Wane, who led the Warriors to multiple championships during his tenure.

The Cherry and White

The Wigan Warriors’ official colors are cherry (red) and white, which are proudly worn by fans and players alike.

Wigan Warriors Women

The club now has a women’s team, providing opportunities for female players to showcase their skills at the highest level.
