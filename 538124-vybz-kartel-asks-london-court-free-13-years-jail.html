Vybz Kartel Asks London Court to Free Him after Over 13 Years in Jail

June 2024 · 3 minute read
  • Dancehall music legend Vybz Kartel appealed his conviction at the Privy Council, Jamaica's final appellate court
  • Kartel was sentenced to life imprisonment with the possibility for parole after serving a minimum of 35 years
  • The singer's lawyer, Isat Buchanan argued for his release, citing health issues and alleged jury tampering

Incarcerated Jamaican dancehall star Vybz Kartel has asked a London court to quash his 2014 murder conviction.

What did Vybz Kartel's lawyer say?

Through his lawyers, Kartel told the Privy Council in London that he was wrongly convicted for the murder of his associate, Clive Williams, alias Lizard.

The Privy Council is Jamaica's final appellate court.

It granted Kartel an expedited hearing last week following concerns about his heart condition and battle with Graves’ disease.

Kartel was arrested in 2011 over the disappearance of Lizard and was convicted in 2014 along with three others; Kahiro Jones, Andre St John and Shawn Campbell.

The Summer Time hitmaker was sentenced to life imprisonment with the possibility for parole once he has served a minimum of 35 years in prison. The sentence was later reduced on appeal to 32 and a half years.

According to the prosecution, Lizard was killed in August 2011, at Kartel's house in Havendale, St Andrew, upon the orders of the self-styled 'Worl' Boss'.

This is after Lizard and another man lost two guns that Kartel had bestowed on them for safekeeping. Lizard's body has never been found.

Kartel, however, argues that there were irregularities in the trial, including jury tampering.

The appeal is being heard on February 14 and February 15 with a ruling expected at a later date.
