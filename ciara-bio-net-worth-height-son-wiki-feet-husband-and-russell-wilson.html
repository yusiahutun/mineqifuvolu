Ciara is many things to many people, but in general, she is an African American singer-songwriter, singer, producer, model, and actress. As a songwriter, she became professionally known in 2003. Since then this musical diva has released several albums (six studio albums) and many hit singles. Since the beginning of her career, Ciara has won numerous music awards, including the Grammy Awards. She has also introduced musicians like Ludacris, Justin Timberlake, Missy Elliot, Fatman Scoop and so many others. Her net worth is about 20 million dollars.

Bio: Early Life and Career Beginnings

Ciara, whose birth name is Ciara Princess Harris, was born on 25 October 1985 in Austin, Texas, in the USA as the daughter of her parents Jackie and Carlton Clay Harris. Her father was an army officer and her mother an air force officer. Ciara spent her childhood traveling between countries, cities, and towns – Germany, New York, Utah, California, Arizona, and Nevada – until the family decided to settle in Georgia. She attended Riverdale High School in Riverdale, Georgia.

As an only child, she had the freedom to choose a career of her choice, and as a child, she wanted to be a model. But she would change that career path after seeing a musical show on television. She decided to become a musician and formed a singing group with her friends that she called “Hearsay”. The group recorded some songs but later disbanded due to irreconcilable differences between them. Ciara decided to continue her music career as a lone fighter and met the music producer Jazze Pha, who helped her get her very first contract with Laface Records in 2002. She wrote her first song Got Me Waiting for R&B singer, Fantasia Barrino.

In 2004 she released her first single Goodie. The song went platinum and exceeded her expectations. Since then she has released more hit singles that have topped the US music charts. She immersed herself in acting, which was very successful. She starred in movies such as All You’ve Got, (2006) Mama, I Want To Sing (2012) and That’s My Boy (2012).
