Guns N' Roses' tour bus breaks down on the way to Coachella Music Festival

March 2024 Â· 3 minute read

Guns N' Roses' Coachella gig got off to a bumpy start when the bands' tour bus broke down on the way to pick them up.

The rockers - Axl Rose, lead guitarist Slash and bass player Duff McKagan - were left waiting for a ride when the bus broke down for three hours on the way to Indio, California, reportsTMZ.

The band are headlining on Saturday at the three-day music festival.

Bump in the road: Guns N' Roses' tour bus reportedly broke down and was stuck on the side of the highway for three hours on the way to pick up the rockers for their Coachella gig on Saturday

The bus, emblazoned with 'Welcome To The Jungle' on the side, broke down on The 10 freeway and sat by the side of the road for three hours before getting repaired and heading on its way, reports TMZ.

The legendary rockers are having some bad luck since they reunited for a surprise live show in Los Angeles on April 2 and kicked off a massive North American stadium tour.

It was the band's first live show together since 1993.

But front man Axl, 54, broke his foot and had to have surgery after their Los Angeles gig.

Back together: Axl performed in a cast as he joined bandmates Duff McKagan, left, and Slash, onstage as they kicked off their stadium reunion tour in Las Vegas on April 9

He had to be pushed on stage in a wheelchair when they kicked off their reunion stadium tour in Las Vegas on April 9, and performed sitting in a 'rock throne' borrowed from The Foo Fighters' Dave Grohl.

The rocker tweeted a video from his doctor about his broken foot, joking 'This is what can happen when you do something you haven't done in nearly over 23 years.'

The iconic hard rockers shot to fame with their 1987 album Appetite for Destruction, which remains the best-selling debut album of all time.

But they split over bad blood between Axl and Slash, with the singer calling the guitarist 'a cancer' at one point.

Broken foot: The singer joked about fracturing his foot after the band's surprise reunion show in LA earlier this month

Ouch: The 54-year-old shared a video after having surgery on his foot earlier this month

Axl - who has kept playing as Guns N' Roses with other guitarists - also shunned the band's induction into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2012.

Their reunion tour is set to earn the band millions.

Music industry magazine Billboard estimated that Guns N 'Roses would earn $3 million per arena show, and much more at Coachella, which draws more than 180,000 people a year over two consecutive three-day weekends with identical lineups. 

On Friday, LCD Soundsystem also reunited for a headline show at the festival, paying tribute to Guns N' Roses with a cover of November Rain.
