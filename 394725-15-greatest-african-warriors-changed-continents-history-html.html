15 greatest African warriors who changed the continent's history

June 2024 · 7 minute read

Historical accounts of African warfare and warriors are often regarded as folk tales, but are they? In the current society, the warrior ethos has been romanticised on the cinema front, and it continues to linger. Various campaigns use the warrior moniker to express fierceness. But the stories aren't folk tales, and they represent actual personalities that altered the continent's history. Who are the greatest African warriors celebrated by different societies and nations?

Tales are told of the unearthly might and sheer determination of African leaders who altered the continent's trajectory. Their extraordinary abilities to tear enemies apart are celebrated to this day. They are hailed as the true African heroes that liberated or at least attempted to free their people from external influence.

Renowned African warriors

Without a doubt, Africa is a region with a remarkable and glorious history, laden with distinct cultures and traditions. From the Zulus in the South to the Egyptians in the North, every culture has produced profound warriors and great leaders. So, who are the fascinating figures in African history revered as legendary warriors and rulers?

1. Ahmed Aba

Ahmed Alba was the leader of the Songhai Empire that is present-day Niger, Nothern Nigeria, Senegal, Gambia, Guinea, Mauritania, and Mali. The African warrior ruled over two-thirds of West Africa.

Although he was a renowned and fierce leader, Ahmed, formally known as Abu al-Abbas Ahmed al-Takrui Al-Massufi al-Tinbukti was highly educated, and a great political provocateur. His empire collapsed after the invasion of the Europeans and Arabs in 1591. He died in the Sahara in 1627.

2. Behanzin Hossu Bowelle (The King Shark)

Behanzin is famous for outpowering the French expedition in 1890 and forcing them to pay for utilising the port of Cotonou. Indeed, he was one of the most powerful and fearsome African warriors who fought slavery. He ruled during the last years of the 19th century.

However, Behanzin was defeated in 1894 by a Senegalese-European known as Colonel As. A. Dodds. The colonel was given a powerful French army to help him defeat the great African warrior. Behanzin perished in 1906.

3. Almamy Suluku

Almamy ruled the largest kingdom in Sierra Leone, Biriwa, managing to maintain the region's independence and prevent European influence. He even became a war captain and expanded his kingdom. That is why he is considered one of the greatest African warriors ever to rule.

4. Cetshwayo Kampande

If you are looking for the deadliest African warriors, then Cetshwayo Kampande is your guy. He was a great war hero that defeated the British in one of their biggest defeats ever in the hands of Africans.

Cetshwayo was a Zulu King that resisted the British's invasion to Zululand and even killed the French heir to the throne, Prince Napoleon. The African legend was finally captured and perished in February 1844.

5. Ezana of Aksum

Ezana is an Eritrean leader that succeeded his father (Ella Amida) while still a child. The African warrior created numerous successful military campaigns in his country that made them stronger. Ezana is also recognised for being the first Eritrean leader to embrace Christianity.

6. Yaa Asantewaa

Yaa Asantewaa makes the list of renowned female African warriors. She was a Ghanaian warrior queen, born into the Ashanti Kingdom. Her greatest achievement that is celebrated until today is her conquest of the British. This defeat allowed her people to resist invasion. Yaa was later exiled in Seychelles where she resided until her death.

7. The Dahomey Amazons

There are different types of African warriors, and the Dahomey Amazons create a unique group. They were female African warriors that were known to be ferocious fighters and had a reputation of decapitating soldiers during battle.

They represented the Kingdom of Dahomey, which is nowadays’ Republic of Benin. The female warriors fought alongside male soldiers in 1890 in the First Franco-Dahomean war against the French. Their impressive skills and remarkable agility allowed the warriors to overwhelm the French troops.

8. Nzinga of Ndongo and Matamba

How many ancient rulers and warriors do you know of today's' Angola? Nzinga was a smart leader of the Ndongo region and Matamba Kingdoms of the Mbundu people. She ruled the region in the 17th century.

The African warrior was very tactical and intelligent in her strategies. She created a great army by giving runaway slaves and Portuguese-trained African soldiers sanctuary. This strong force allowed her to resist numerous Portuguese colonisation attempts.

9. Bai Bureh

Bai Bureh was a great military strategist that led the Temme and Loko uprising against the British in 1898. This was in Northern Sierra Leone. The ruler was angered by the British imposing HUT Tax that forced residents to pay taxes in the form of money, labour, grain and stock.

Bureh refused this tax policy and declared war on the British. He led his troops that fought vigorously for months killing numerous British troops. Unfortunately, he surrendered and was exiled to Ghana. Bureh later returned to Sierra Leone where he became the Chief of Kasseh.

10. Shaka kaSenzangakhona

Shaka is perhaps the most renowned and recognised African warrior in history. He was a great leader of the Zulu Kingdom. He is famous for uniting the Nguni people into his Zulu Kingdom. This union allowed him to rule over a massive portion of Southern Africa.

Shaka was a great statesman and a military genius. The African warrior’s reign was marked by reforms and brutality in equal measure. However, Shaka was assassinated by his two half-brothers, Dingane and Mhlangana.

11. Samori Ture

He was born in Sanankoro, a village in the Southeast of Kankan that is present-day Guinea. This African warrior preferred confrontation, whereby he utilised warfare and diplomacy to handle the French that was colonising the West African region. This resistance established Samori Ture as the leading African opponent of European imperialism.

12. Sonni Ali

Sonni Ali was the Sonni dynasty 15th consecutive ruler in 1464. He was educated in the methods of warfare that made him a fierce African warrior. During his first years as the ruler of the Songhai Empire, Sonni conquered several neighbours. This included the capture of Timbucktoo that was occupied by the Tuareg.

According to the Songhai oral tradition, Sonni Ali was a great military commander. However, certain Islamic chronicles put him as an unscrupulous tyrant. Regardless of the narrative that you believe, it is evident that he had a great impact in the region, helping in altering the course of the African continent.

13. Amina

Amina’s 34-year reign of the Zazzau Kingdom, Zaria in present-day Nigeria ensured the safe passage of traders in the entire Saharan region. She is amongst the most popular African warrior queens that instilled various developments in her region. These included introducing metal battle armour, the building of military encampments behind fortress walls and many more.

14. Sarraounia Mangou

Sarraounia Mangou resisted French invasion of her territory. She was an African female chief that led her troops against the French in the Battle of Logou (present-day Niger) in 1899. Although her troops were overwhelmed, the female African warrior engaged in guerrilla tactics that averted her capture.

15. Sundiata Keita

Sundiata Keita is renowned as the founder of the Ancient Mali Empire (The Gambia, Guinea, Guinea-Bissau, Ivory Coast, Mali, Niger, Mauritania, and Senegal) in the 13th century. His kingdom became one of the richest in the world. Sundiata Keita even declared the first charter of human rights, the Manden Charter.
